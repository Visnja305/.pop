Brand Voice & Persona

Understanding the archetype, personality, and tone that drives our global message.

Core Brand Archetype

The Innocent

"Bringing simple happiness, nostalgic comfort, and optimistic celebration to everyday lives."

We speak not from a position of superiority, but of shared connection. We focus on common human values, refreshing the body and uplifting the spirit with simple, direct messaging.

Optimistic

Always focusing on the positive, active side of life. Our campaigns invite participation, celebrate friendship, and look to a unified future.

Authentic

Warm, honest, and grounded in heritage. We speak to real people in real-life scenarios, eschewing cold corporate jargon or hyper-technical claims.

Inclusive (Universal)

Coca-Cola belongs to everyone. Our language is simple, clear, and translated across cultures without losing its essential sense of welcoming togetherness.

Refreshing

Crisp, sensory, and active. We use words that evoke visual imagery, taste, and physical invigoration: pop, fizz, ice-cold, crisp, and sparkling.

Typography System

How we pair class-leading serif headlines with precise, clean body sans-serif forms.

Playfair Display

Headings & Editorial
  • Weights: Bold (700), Extra Bold (800)
  • Letter Spacing: -0.015em (Tight)
  • Usage: Main titles, hero statements, large editorial highlights.

Inter

Body & Metadata
  • Weights: Regular (400), Medium (500), Semi-Bold (600)
  • Letter Spacing: Normal, or +0.06em (Uppercase Labels)
  • Usage: Body paragraphs, specifications, interface controls, buttons.

Brand Assets & Ribbon

Guidelines surrounding the script mark, alignment, and the iconic Coca-Cola Dynamic Ribbon Device.

  • Script Logo Clear Space

    A minimum boundary of space must surround the logo at all times. This clear space is proportional to the height of the capital letter "C" in the wordmark.

  • The Dynamic Ribbon Device

    The fluid, sweeping wave represents the physical motion of liquid. In modern digital assets, the white ribbon sits beneath the script mark to reinforce visual energy.

  • Red Canvas Domination

    Our brand red should occupy at least 60% of brand touchpoints. Digital mockups use Cherry Black backgrounds to establish luxury, but retain red for focal highlights.
